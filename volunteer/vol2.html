Volunteer

Good Neighbors

2021 South Korea

Overview

I was a member of '모티 1기' (Moti 1st Generation), a university student volunteer club under Good Neighbors (굿네이버스) Gyeonggi Northern Branch. The program focused on digital literacy education for digitally vulnerable populations — visiting senior homes and orphanages biweekly to teach basic technology skills.

Activities

  • Biweekly visits to senior homes and orphanages — Visited care facilities every two weeks to provide hands-on digital education to elderly residents and children
  • Kiosk and smartphone training — Taught seniors how to use public kiosks (ordering food, purchasing tickets, accessing government services) and basic smartphone operations
  • Computer basics education — Guided participants through fundamental computer skills including internet browsing, messaging, and document handling
  • Patient, personalized instruction — Adapted teaching pace and methods to each individual’s comfort level, often repeating and simplifying explanations for those with no prior digital experience

Impact & Reflection

This experience taught me the value of patience and meeting people where they are. Teaching technology to seniors who had never touched a smartphone required me to break down complex concepts into the simplest possible steps and adjust my communication style entirely. It also reinforced that technology only has value when people can actually use it — a perspective that continues to shape how I think about building accessible, user-centered systems.
